As Edward and Karl depart Ashton, the townsfolk's festive atmosphere gives way to a sense of detachment; only Don Price's scowling face betrays his animosity towards Edward's departure. The crowd's hasty farewells and well-wishes are tempered by the Old Woman's enigmatic parting words, which leave Edward perplexed and uncertain about the value of her cryptic advice - as they walk away from Ashton, the once-cheery scene dissolves into an uneasy silence, casting a shadow over Edward's uncertain future.
