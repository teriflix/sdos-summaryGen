INT. GUEST BEDROOM - NIGHT

Edward lies asleep on his back as Josephine quietly lowers the shade and switches off the radio. As she sits beside him, he stirs, recalling a childhood dream about an ominous crow predicting family member deaths. He tells her of how his father's subsequent anxiety after hearing the crow's warning is eerily foreshadowed by the unrelated death of the milkman, who was supplying their mother with extra cream. The conversation devolves into laughter as Edward embellishes the tale, but a flash of pain reveals the weight of a long-buried secret: that his father and stepmother were never meant to be together, a truth he's kept hidden even from Josephine.
