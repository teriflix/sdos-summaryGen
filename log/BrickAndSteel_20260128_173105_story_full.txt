STORY SUMMARY REPORT

=== SYNOPSIS ===
In a world where the lines between leisure and purpose are blurred, Jack's attempt to collect on his father's insurance policy hits a snag when he confronts Rachel, the cold and calculating representative of the company. As she digs through the stacks of paperwork, her detached demeanor forces Jack to confront the harsh truth that his claims may be met with resistance rather than compassion, leaving him feeling defeated and vulnerable.

Meanwhile, in a tranquil scene on Brick's patio, Dick Steel's arrival brings a spark of warmth and camaraderie with his friend, but as their shared laughter shatters, it reveals a deeper restlessness that neither can shake off. Their unspoken admission that retirement has been a disappointment sets them on a collision course with the harsh realities they've been trying to outrun.

As Jack's growing unease about his own future fuels an argument with Dan in their makeshift lair, the widening chasm between their differing visions for freedom and permanence threatens to erode their partnership. This fissure is further widened by Steel's revelation that Point Blank Sniper is more than just a myth, forcing him to confront the danger he's always tried to keep at bay.

Cognito's world is turned upside down as he's brutalized by Brick and Steel in his ramshackle hideout, his defiant facade cracking to reveal a glimmer of fear behind the bravado. As he spills his deepest secret - a list of deadly adversaries converging on Brick and Steel - their ruthless duo becomes even more merciless, leaving Cognito's fragile grip on life on the brink of collapse.

As they peel out of the garage, Brick and Steel embark on a full-throttle quest for freedom that dissolves their carefully constructed facade of retirement. Their reckless pursuit is matched by the Handsome Man's growing desperation as his enemies are discovered, exposing a deeper weakness behind his opulent facade.

In this complex web of alliances and rivalries, characters are forced to confront the harsh realities they've been trying to outrun, leaving them with an unbridgeable gap between their promises and consequences.

=== CHARACTER PROFILES ===

--- STEEL ---
Steel is a battle-hardened, midlife-crisis-ridden veteran of sorts, whose gruff demeanor and wisecracking humor betray a deep-seated anxiety about being left behind by time's relentless march. His banter with Beer is a desperate attempt to cling to youth and camaraderie, even as the world around him seems to be catching up. Steel's laconic responses, though often laced with humor, belie a darker introspection - a sense of being "put away" like so many others, left to rust in the scrapheap of life. His unease is amplified by the sudden appearance of threats, which send him into a reactive, adrenaline-fueled frenzy, as if trying to outrun his own obsolescence. Steel's motivations are a tangled web of nostalgia for past glories and a desperate desire to stay relevant; his relationships with Beer and others are marked by a power struggle between mentorship and rivalry. As he speeds off into the unknown, Steel is propelled by a mix of fear, aggression, and a stubborn refusal to be written off - but whether this will lead him to redemption or further chaos remains to be seen.

--- BRICK ---
Brick, the 35-year-old high school English teacher, exudes an air of introverted melancholy, his words often trailing off into wry asides and half-hearted declarations that belie a deep-seated discontent with his own life. His relationships are marked by a crippling awkwardness, as if he's forever stuck in a perpetual state of adolescence, unable to shake the feeling that he's been "put away" like so many of his fellow classmates. The facade of humor he maintains serves as a defense against the crushing existential dread that threatens to consume him whole, particularly when confronted with the prospect of growing old and losing relevance - an idea he vehemently rejects, substituting it instead with a desperate desire to recapture the vitality and idealism of youth. His inner turmoil simmers just below the surface, boiling over into despairing silences and desperate attempts to connect with others, only to find himself isolated by his own self-imposed barriers. As he grapples with the abyss that stares back at him from the mirror, Brick's voice becomes a stammering attempt to drown out the cacophony of fears that threaten to engulf him, his words tumbling forth in a mad dash for meaning and validation in a world that seems determined to exclude him.

--- COGNITO ---
Cognito, a man beset on all sides by the chaos of his own circumstances, navigates the treacherous landscape with a mix of bravado and vulnerability. His quick wit and sharp tongue are defensive mechanisms masking a deep-seated anxiety that threatens to overwhelm him at every turn, as evidenced by the frantic cadence of his dialogue and the desperate attempt to deflect the threats hurled at him by Brick and Steel. Beneath the swagger lies a man struggling to maintain control in a world that seems determined to strip him bare, with a psyche ravaged by the relentless pursuit of those who would do him harm - a condition exacerbated by the seemingly endless roll call of adversaries: Scorpio, The Boy Band, Sparrow, Point Blank Sniper. As he faces off against his tormentors, Cognito's fractured personality, oscillating between resilience and despair, is laid bare in the fleeting glance shared with Brick and Steel, an unmistakable sign that this fragile facade may soon give way to a maelstrom of fear and desperation.

--- DAN ---
Dan is a midlife crisis incarnate, a 50-something man whose worn façade and sharp tongue betray a deep-seated sense of desperation and disconnection. He's a relic of a bygone era, clinging to the remnants of a long-lost identity as a powerful player in his own life story, but now reduced to mere spectator status in a world that has moved on without him. His flinty exterior - a product of years spent navigating the cutthroat world of business and politics - cracks with every failed endeavor, revealing a seething cauldron of anxiety and insecurity beneath. Dan's goading of Jack is a cry for validation, a thinly veiled attempt to reassert his relevance in a world that no longer seems to need him; his use of the word "permanently" suggests a man who has reached the end of his tether, exhausted from a lifetime of struggle and determined to take one final swipe at the status quo before he's left with nothing but regret.

--- HANDSOME MAN ---
As he stands at the precipice of adulthood, Handsome Man's life is a tangled web of privilege and entitlement, with his smooth façade cracking to reveal a cauldron of unresolved anger and insecurity. His loud outbursts - "I want them dead. DEAD!" - are a desperate cry for control in a world that's begun to slip through his fingers, where the only constant is the fleeting nature of pleasure and the crushing weight of responsibility. But beneath this bravado lies a man consumed by the same vacuity he mocks with his beer-soaked antics, a numbing escape from the existential dread that threatens to engulf him - "Beer flies." This is a character defined by contradictions: the tough exterior hiding a fragile sense of self, the reckless abandon masking a deep-seated fear of being trapped or tied down. As he careens through life on a collision course with his own demons, Handsome Man's greatest struggle may not be the external world, but the internal battle to reconcile his desire for freedom with the crushing need for connection and belonging.

=== SCENE BREAKDOWN ===
SCENE 0: START
In the dimly lit, cramped offices of a small insurance company, Jack's desperate attempt to collect on his father's policy hits a snag as the cold, calculating gaze of the company's representative, Rachel, makes him realize he's not as prepared for this confrontation as he thought. As she digs through the stacks of paperwork, her detached demeanor and unyielding scrutiny threaten to strip away the carefully constructed narrative Jack has built around his father's death, forcing him to confront the harsh truth that his claims may be met with resistance rather than compassion. The air is thick with tension as Jack's composure begins to fray, and for a moment, Rachel's mask slips, revealing a glimmer of something like pity, but it's quickly suppressed, leaving Jack feeling defeated and vulnerable.

SCENE 1: EXT. BRICK'S PATIO - DAY
On Brick's patio, a tranquil scene unfolds as Dick Steel's arrival brings a spark of warmth and camaraderie. Their shared laughter and raised beers in a toast to retirement momentarily mask the weight of their post-service lives, but as the silence stretches, the men's eyes lock, and the levity shatters, revealing a deeper restlessness that neither can shake off; together they utter a defiant phrase that cuts through the facade of relaxation, signaling an unspoken admission that retirement has been a disappointment, and the line between leisure and purpose is blurred.

SCENE 2: INT. TRAILER HOME - DAY
As the haze of success and youthful invincibility hangs heavy over their makeshift lair, Dan's zeal for anarchy clashes with Jack's growing unease, a widening chasm fueled by the mention of Brick and Steel's retirement - a status that now seems within reach. The latter's impassioned counterpoint in Vietnamese underscores his emotional investment in preserving their freedom, but Dan's resolute gaze remains fixed on permanence, emboldened by their lucrative exploits. The argument swirls like a maelstrom, with neither side yielding, yet the undertow of Jack's trepidation slowly begins to erode the foundation of their partnership, leaving an unbridgeable gap between the promise of glory and the reality of consequence.

SCENE 3: EXT. BRICK'S POOL - DAY
As Steel's heated phone call reaches a boiling point, his defenses are breached by the impossible reality staring back at him from the sniper scope - a face inches away, eerily familiar yet unmistakably deadly. The revelation that Point Blank Sniper is more than just a myth crashes through Steel's skepticism like a tidal wave, forcing him to confront the very thing he's been trying to outrun. One look into those crosshairs and his bravado shatters, leaving only a stunned acknowledgment of the danger he's always tried to keep at bay - a stark contrast to the polished public image being presented in the opening titles that follow, as if the world outside this moment is frozen in time while Steel's entire world unravels.

SCENE 4: EXT. WOODEN SHACK - DAY
In the gritty, sun-drenched yard of his ramshackle hideout, Cognito's world is turned upside down as he's brutalized by the ruthless duo, Brick and Steel, their faces twisted with an unnerving intensity. As they pummel him against the wooden shack, Cognito's defiant facade cracks, revealing a glimmer of fear behind the bravado. The sudden shift in dynamics forces Cognito to spill his deepest secret: a list of deadly adversaries converging on Brick and Steel, his words tumbling out in a frantic bid for survival. The look shared between the pair is a cold, calculating spark that ignites a dread within Cognito - he knows that with this information, Brick and Steel's wrath will be merciless, and his fragile grip on life is about to slip irreparably.

SCENE 5: INT. GARAGE - DAY
As they peel out of the garage, the tension in Brick's body dissolves into a mix of fear and exhilaration, while Steel's grin reveals a glint of recklessness that masks a deeper desperation - their quest for freedom is now a full-throttle rush into the unknown, leaving behind the carefully constructed facade of retirement, and with it, the illusion of a life untethered to Mom's legacy.

SCENE 6: EXT. PALATIAL MANSION - DAY
As the Handsome Man's composure unravels, his desire for control intensifies, fueled by the news of his enemies' discovery, but his rage is matched only by his own weakness, revealed in his careless disposal of the beer and subsequent outburst, which instead of commanding respect, exposes a deeper desperation, as he becomes increasingly isolated behind his opulent facade.
